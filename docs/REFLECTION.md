# Reflection – Student Assignment Tracker

## Overview

This reflection outlines the challenges encountered during the requirements engineering process for the Student Assignment Tracker system, particularly in identifying stakeholders and balancing their needs.

---

## Challenges in Identifying Stakeholders

One of the main challenges was identifying all relevant stakeholders involved in the system. While primary users such as students and lecturers were easy to identify, it required deeper analysis to recognize additional stakeholders such as system administrators, IT support staff, and university management.

These stakeholders play a critical role in ensuring the system operates effectively, even though they do not interact with it directly on a daily basis.

---

## Conflicting Stakeholder Needs

Another challenge was balancing conflicting stakeholder requirements. For example:

* Students prefer real-time notifications and fast system responses
* IT support staff prioritize system stability and resource efficiency
* University administration requires detailed reporting and data accuracy

These needs can conflict, particularly in terms of system performance versus resource usage.

---

## Trade-offs in System Design

Designing the system required making trade-offs between usability, performance, and scalability. For instance:

* Implementing real-time notifications improves user experience but increases system load
* Supporting a large number of concurrent users requires more scalable infrastructure
* Ensuring strong security measures may slightly impact system performance

These trade-offs were carefully considered to ensure the system remains practical and feasible for implementation.

---

## Conclusion

The requirements engineering process highlighted the importance of understanding stakeholder perspectives and balancing competing priorities. This exercise improved my ability to think critically about system design and ensured that the proposed solution is both user-focused and technically feasible.
